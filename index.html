Anna Tsvetkov

I’m a Philosophy PhD and Computer Science Sc.M student at Brown. I am also a researcher at MIT FutureTech Lab.

I work with Adam Pautz, Ellie Pavlick, Chris Hill, and David Chalmers (NYU).

My research aims to develop a human-centered approach to AI which integrates philosophical inquiry about the mind with cutting-edge methods in computer science.

The aspiration of my research is that philosophy can guide carefully designed experiments on AI that deepen our understanding of the mind and, in turn, insights about the mind can help us build explainable and ethical AI. My work involves three strands at the core of a human-centered approach to AI:

1. Understanding Artificial and Natural Minds

Can AI represent the world like we do, and how do we design philosophically-informed experiments to compare artificial and natural minds?

Tsvetkov, A. & Pavlick, E. (2024). “Can Large Language Models Represent Perceptual Reality?” (under review)
Tsvetkov, A. (2024). “Troubles with Spatial Functionalism” (under review)
Murphy, R. A., Witnauer, J. E., Castiello, S., Tsvetkov, A., Li, A., Alcaide, D. M., & Miller, R. R. (2022). “More frequent, shorter trials enhance acquisition in a training session: There is a free lunch!” Journal of Experimental Psychology: General.

2. AI as a Tool for Philosophical Progress

Can we use AI to make progress on longstanding philosophical debates about the mind, understanding, and language?

Tsvetkov, A. “Molyneux’s Question and Multimodal Models” (In progress.)

3. The Ethics of AI

What ethical frameworks should guide the development and deployment of AI that align with human values?

Tsvetkov, A., Trisovic, A., Thompson, N. “A Large-Scale Analysis of Ethical Considerations in AI Foundation Models” (In progress with MIT FutureTech Lab.)
Trisovic, A., Tsvetkov, A., et al. “Scientific Use of Foundation Models and Democratization of AI" (In progress with MIT FutureTech Lab.)
